## 1. Market Size & Growth

The Saudi university transport market reached **SAR 1.2 billion** in 2025, with an 18% CAGR (2020-2025). This growth has attracted 25+ platforms — but growth is now decelerating.

### 1.1 Historical Growth

| Year | Market Size (SAR M) | Growth % | Active Platforms |
|------|:-------------------:|:--------:|:----------------:|
| 2020 | 520 | — | 5 |
| 2021 | 610 | +17% | 8 |
| 2022 | 740 | +21% | 12 |
| 2023 | 880 | +19% | 18 |
| 2024 | 1,050 | +19% | 22 |
| 2025 | 1,200 | +14% | 25 |

**Critical observation**: Growth rate began declining (21% → 14%) while platform count continues rising. Classic saturation signal.

### 1.2 Forecast (2026-2028)

Projected growth: 10% (2026), 7% (2027), 5% (2028). Most major universities hold existing 3-5 year contracts. The pool of new clients (private colleges) is limited to ~13 institutions.

## 2. Supply & Demand Analysis

**Demand Side:**
- 58 higher education institutions total (28 public + 30 private)
- 78% (45) already have smart transport solutions
- Only 13 institutions remain without — mostly small, new private colleges
- ~280,000 students use organized transport (42% of total)

**Supply Side:**
- 25+ active platforms
- Average clients per platform: 1.8 universities
- Total platform capacity: covers up to 400,000 students
- **30% capacity surplus** — existing platforms can absorb all expected demand

**56% of all platforms are in Riyadh** (14 platforms), which hosts only 21% of Saudi universities (12). This fatal imbalance means Riyadh-based platforms are cannibalizing each other while regional markets remain underserved but too small to sustain standalone platforms.

## 6. Conclusion

The Saudi university transport market reached SAR 1.2B but has reached maturity. The golden entry window was 2021-2024. Current conditions — decelerating growth, 25+ competitors, declining funding, high entry costs — strongly advise against new platform entry. The market needs consolidation, not new entrants.
